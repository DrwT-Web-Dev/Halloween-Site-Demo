SUBJECT: MISSING PERSON / UNATTENDED DEATH
───────────────────────────────────────────────

SUMMARY:
At approximately 9:40 P.M. on OCTOBER 21, 1974, a call was received
from one BELINDA WELLS (F, 43) of [REDACTED] Township reporting her
husband, FREDRICH WELLS (M, 47), missing from their property on
COUNTY ROUTE [REDACTED]. Mrs. Wells stated her husband had last been
seen tending the pumpkin fields during dense fog conditions earlier
that evening.

A search effort was organized the following morning with local
volunteers and township deputies. The search was impeded by
visibility under ten feet and was ultimately suspended after
a severe storm front moved over the area. Subject remained missing.

───────────────────────────────────────────────
FOLLOW-UP:
On OCTOBER 31, 1974, Mrs. Wells contacted neighbors regarding a
"foul odor" emanating from the shed behind the residence. Witnesses
reported seeing her enter the structure shortly before dusk.
No further contact was made with Mrs. Wells.

On NOVEMBER 14, 1974, Deputies responded to the property to conduct
a welfare check after Mrs. Wells failed to answer calls or collect
mail. Entry was gained through an unlocked rear door.

Inside the shed, officers discovered one deceased female, later
confirmed to be BELINDA WELLS, lying prone on the floor, left hand
clutching a 12-gauge shotgun registered to F. Wells.

On a worktable approximately four feet from the body was a human
head, identified through dental records as belonging to FREDRICH
WELLS. The head showed deliberate mutilation. Eyes and mouth had
been excised and carved into a grin resembling a jack-o'-lantern.
Despite the elapsed time since the subject’s disappearance,
tissue showed minimal decomposition. Beside the remains was a
Halloween card containing the message [REDACTED]
